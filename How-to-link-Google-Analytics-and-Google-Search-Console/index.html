    How to link Google Analytics and Google Search Console

    February 28, 2020

    In another post I described how to register your Minimal Mistakes website with Google Analytics and Google Search Console. It turns out that I missed one crucial step - linking them together. Here’s a HOWTO do it.    

    Why linking Google Search Console with Google Analytics?

    Google Search Console captures queries that people use to find your website in Google. In otherwords, the keywords that drive organic traffic to your site. It also provides major metrics relevant for SEO:

    • Total Clicks
    • Total impressions
    • Average Click-Trough-Rate (CTR)
    • Average position (in Google Search)

    By linking with Google Analytics, you will have all these data in Acquisition –> Search Console tab, which is handy.

    Connecting Google Search Console with Google Analytics also enabled Analytics Intelligence - ML-generated insights in dashboards-like format to better understand trends and user behavior on your site 1:

     

    URL-prefix verification

    At first, I couldn’t figure out why I get the message “You haven’t verified any sites”. I thought I did - since I have my Google Search Console running. It turned out that when you first registered with Google Search Console, you went through domain verfication process. But to link it with Google Analytics, you need to go through URL-prefix verification process first.    

    For this, go to your Google Search Console and add another property (in the left top corner):

     

    Then click to add URL-prefix property and type your website’s URL.

    After that go to your Google Analytics account and click on Settings in the left down corner. Choose from the PRODUCT LINKING (middle pane) “All Products”. Scroll down to “Search Console”:

    Now you can enable the Google Search Console data in Google Analytics:
